You come upon a quiet forest lake. The water of the lake is very clear, you see small pebbles on the sandy bottom, water plants waving in a soft current, and small fish swimming about. In the centre of the lake lies a small green island. Several weathered stepping stones lead from the forest shore towards the island.

In the middle of the island stands a stone statue. It appears to be the statue of a woman. Beautifully carved, the woman seems almost alive. She is clad in a flowing gown, finely chiselled curls fall down her face and shoulders. She seems to smile, but you cannot be sure.

As you watch the statue, you feel a calm come over you. Her stone eyes seem to look directly into yours, and as they do so, you feel as if for a moment, a sparkle of sunlight has entered your thoughts, driving away your troubles.

Suddenly, you hear leaves rustling behind you. ‘Quiet.’ a soft voice whispers. ‘They’ll hear us.’ As you turn, you see two bandits stealthily emerge from the forest. They are clad in dark brown leather, and wear green hooded mantles. Their faces are rugged and unclean, and bear the mark of many a vicious battle. They have unsheathed sharp looking daggers.

‘Look what you have done.’ one of them whispers. Then he addresses you. ‘Welcome, friends, to our beautiful lake. For this wonderful view, we ask only all your gold and possessions. Or else, your life.’

As you refuse, the bandit smiles. ‘That’s what I thought. We will have to take it from your dead bodies then.’ The bandits attack.

The bandits, having obviously met their match, lie defeated on the forest floor, their days of thieving and villainy at an end.
